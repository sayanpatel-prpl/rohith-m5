Companies with pricing power (Symphony, TTK Prestige) are better positioned to recover; those with weak brands or distribution (Bajaj, IFB) will struggle.

**Outlier**: Bajaj is the only company that crossed into operating loss territory (Q3 FY26 PBT -Rs 20.28 Cr). This is not cyclical — it's structural distress.

## 6. Sector Themes Summary

### Theme 1: The BEE Transition Window (Q4 FY26 — FY27)
BEE star label ratcheting on ACs (Jan 2026) and fans creates a **one-time 7-10% pricing reset opportunity**. Companies that pair this with cost optimization will structurally improve margins. Companies that merely pass through BOM increases will see temporary relief absorbed by the next commodity cycle. **Advisory opportunity**: Cost-to-serve optimization timed with BEE pricing reset.

### Theme 2: The Great Margin Compression of FY26
Weak summer + copper/aluminium inflation + labor code provisions created sector-wide margin pain. But the compression is not uniform — it's exposing which companies have structural cost problems (Bajaj, IFB) versus cyclical headwinds (Crompton, Blue Star). **Advisory opportunity**: Differentiate structural distress from cyclical weakness for PE/M&A targeting.

### Theme 3: Distribution Is the New Moat
The companies gaining share (Crompton, Orient, TTK Prestige) all have distinctive distribution innovations — Crompton's e-commerce dominance, Orient's DTM model, TTK's Xclusive stores. The companies losing share (Bajaj, Voltas) have distribution problems — Bajaj losing retailers, Voltas's ISD scaling challenges. **Advisory opportunity**: Channel transformation consulting.

### Theme 4: Component Backward Integration Race
Amber, Dixon, Havells, and Voltas are all investing in backward integration (PCBs, compressors, cables, components). The companies that achieve component self-sufficiency first will have structural margin advantages. Blue Star's refusal to backward-integrate compressors is the most contrarian bet in the sector. **Advisory opportunity**: Make-vs-buy advisory; JV structuring.

### Theme 5: South-to-Pan-India Expansion
TTK Prestige, Butterfly, V-Guard, and Orient are all South India-dominant companies executing pan-India expansion. The playbooks differ — V-Guard is adding 5K partners/year, Orient is doing DTM conversions, TTK is opening Xclusive stores. **Advisory opportunity**: Go-to-market strategy for geographic expansion.
