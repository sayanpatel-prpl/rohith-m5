Below is a practical, executive-grade structure for an AI-driven monthly Consumer & Retail Mid-Market Intelligence Newsletter (₹1,000–10,000 Cr revenue), followed by the key parameters your AI should track.  
---

## **1\. Cover & Executive Snapshot (1 page)**

Purpose: Give you a 5-minute skim that answers “What changed this month and why should I care?”

Sections

* Month in 5 Bullets  
  * 2–3 financial shifts  
  * 1–2 operational / cost / supply chain signals  
* Big Themes Emerging  
  * e.g., “Gross margin pressure easing in staples”, “Aggressive regional expansion in QSR”, “Private labels gaining shelf share”  
* Red Flags / Watchlist  
  * Companies or sub-segments showing stress

AI Output

* Auto-generated summary with signal \> noise filtering  
* Confidence score on insights (high / medium)

---

## **2\. Market Pulse – Consumer & Retail Mid-Market**

Purpose: Macro \+ sector trends, but only what impacts mid-market players.

Coverage

* Demand Signals  
  * Volume vs price growth  
  * Urban vs semi-urban consumption  
* Input Cost Trends  
  * Key commodities (edible oil, dairy, packaging, logistics)  
* Margin Outlook  
  * Gross margin expansion/contraction trends  
* Channel Mix Shifts  
  * GT vs MT vs e-commerce vs D2C

Key Parameters

* Volume growth %  
* Price hikes / rollbacks  
* Gross margin trend (↑ ↓ →)  
* Channel contribution shifts

---

## **3\. Financial Performance Tracker (Company-Level)**

Purpose: Quickly see who is winning, who is struggling, and why.

Universe

* 30–50 tracked companies in:  
  * FMCG  
  * QSR / Food Services  
  * Apparel & Lifestyle  
  * Consumer Durables  
  * Beauty & Personal Care  
  * Omni-channel retail

Standardized Metrics (Very Important)

* Revenue growth (YoY / QoQ)  
* EBITDA margin  
* Working capital days  
* Inventory days  
* Net debt / EBITDA  
* Capex intensity  
* ROCE trend

AI Layer

* Variance analysis vs:  
  * Last quarter  
  * Segment average  
* “Outperform / Inline / Underperform” tagging  
* Short explanation in 2–3 lines

---

## **4\. Deals, Transactions & Capital Movements**

Purpose: Track where money and control are moving.

Coverage

* M\&A (majority & minority)  
* PE / VC investments  
* Strategic stake sales  
* Fund raises, IPO filings, QIPs  
* Distressed asset activity

For Each Deal

* Company & segment  
* Deal size & valuation multiple  
* Buyer / Investor profile  
* Strategic rationale (AI-interpreted)  
* Implication for competitors

AI Insight Add-On

* Pattern recognition:  
  * “Multiple investments in regional dairy brands”  
  * “PEs consolidating apparel manufacturing”

---

## **5\. Operational Intelligence & Efficiency Signals**

This is where your newsletter becomes differentiated.

Coverage

* Supply Chain  
  * Localization vs import dependence  
  * Logistics cost trends  
* Manufacturing  
  * Capacity additions / shutdowns  
  * Contract manufacturing shifts  
* Procurement  
  * Vendor consolidation  
  * ZBC / cost take-out initiatives  
* Retail Operations  
  * Store expansion / rationalization  
  * Same-store sales (if available)

Key Parameters

* Inventory turnover  
* Cost as % of sales (logistics, manpower)  
* Capex per store / per MT  
* Store productivity (₹/sqft, if public)
