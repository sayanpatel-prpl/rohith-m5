### 1. Industry Volume Growth vs Company Growth

- **Insight**: Havells delivered 17.2% consolidated revenue growth in FY25 (Rs 21,746 crores vs Rs 18,550 crores), significantly outpacing the broader consumer durables industry. Within segments, Lloyd (consumer electronics/ACs) grew 35.3% YoY, Cables grew 13.7%, and ECD (Electrical Consumer Durables) grew 15.2%. However, in Q1-Q2 FY26, cooling products (ACs, fans, coolers) declined YoY due to an unexpectedly weak summer season, while cables continued accelerating with >20% volume growth. By Q3 FY26, consolidated revenue grew 14% YoY with EBITDA up 21%, indicating operating leverage. Management stated that Havells maintained or gained market share in most categories, with lighting growth better than competition, and water heaters and small domestic appliances (SDA) ahead of industry growth rates. Fans saw mid-single digit degrowth in Q2 FY26. Regional brands gained some ground during hyperinflationary periods, but management expressed confidence that India demographics support long-term FMEG demand.

### 3. Capacity Utilization / Plant Utilization Signals

Wires operate at 65-70% capacity utilization, providing headroom for volume growth. Cables operate at 90-100% capacity utilization, which is the binding constraint — management is doubling cable capacity from FY24 to FY27 and has acquired 39 acres in Alwar for expansion. ### Strategic Signals Summary

**Key Opportunities**:
1. Cables capacity doubling (FY24-FY27) will unlock market share gains in a supply-constrained segment operating at 90-100% utilization
2. Lloyd turnaround: Margin inflection from -4.3% to 2.6% in the fastest-growing segment (35.3%) — further margin expansion to industry norms represents massive profit pool
3. Solar/renewable ecosystem play (Rs 600 crores Goldi investment, EV chargers, battery storage) with revenue target of Rs 1,000-1,500 crores from Rs 420-500 crores base
4. Operating leverage: Stable employee costs (Rs 460-490 crores/quarter for 7 quarters) against 14-17% revenue growth implies structural margin expansion; management guided 150-200bps
5. Real estate cycle recovery: Expected to drive demand across switchgears, cables, lighting, and ECD
6. Rural penetration: Currently 5-6% of consumer revenue but growing faster than urban — significant headroom
7. GST reduction on ACs, TVs, solar — net positive for demand while BEE price hike offset creates consumer-price-neutral transition
8. Premiumization: ~40% BLDC fans, premium appliances, room heaters increasing mix and margins

**Key Risks**:
1. Seasonality and weather dependence: Weak summer in Q1 FY26 caused YoY decline in cooling products — single-season products create binary outcomes
2. Copper price volatility: Copper at Rs 13,000/ton with continuous increases; cables (33% of revenue) directly exposed; pass-through has a time lag
3. Channel inventory build-up: Elevated inventories in Q2 FY26 for cables and Lloyd required tactical schemes — recurring risk in a multi-brand, multi-category model
4. Regional brand competition: Regional brands gaining ground during hyperinflationary periods in FMEG categories
5. US export tariff risk: US exports hit by tariffs; need to diversify export markets
6. Lloyd still at 2.6% margin: Any demand disruption could push it back toward losses; brand building and distribution investment is ongoing cost
7. Attrition at 20.7%: While talent pipeline has been built, attrition remains elevated
8. Exceptional provisions: Rs 45 crores provisioning for new labour codes in Q3 FY26 indicates potential recurring compliance costs
9. Solar margins: Early double digit to high single digit — lower than core segments and could dilute blended margins during scale-up

**Cost Strategy Direction**:
Havells is pursuing a "fixed cost leverage + premiumization" cost strategy.
