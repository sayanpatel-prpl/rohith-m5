Orient Electric Limited, a CK Birla Group Company, received a GST demand order from the Assistant Commissioner of CGST & Central Excise, Indore, amounting to Rs. 33,63,118 with a penalty of Rs. 3,55,546 for the Assessment Year 2020-21. The company received the demand via email on February 27, 2025, and is considering legal options, including filing an appeal. Orient Electric anticipates no significant impact on its financial operations or other activities due to this order.

26th Feb 2025 | 6:04 PM

Orient Electric Limited, a CK Birla Group Company, received a GST demand order from the Office of Assistant Commissioner in EGMORE, North Chennai, citing discrepancies in taxable supply and excess input of IGST for FY 2020-21. The demand amounts to Rs. 13,54,480/- with penalties and interest. The company is planning to appeal against the order and assures no financial impact currently. Ravindra Singh Negi, the Managing Director & CEO, provided the disclosure as per SEBI regulations.

Attendees raised questions regarding employee costs, operating margins, market share trends, direct-to-market strategies, and upcoming plans for specific business units. The management expressed optimism about sustainable growth and operational improvements, indicating a strategic focus on premiumization and strengthening market positions.
