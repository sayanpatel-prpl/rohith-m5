What Changed

Evidence-First Rebuild

We replaced fabricated benchmarks and estimated impact numbers with a rigorous evidence-backed approach. Every claim traces to a source document.

Before
×Fabricated bps impact — "+80 bps Procurement" with no underlying data. Numbers computed from "50% gap closure to sector median"
×Unreliable benchmarks — "Sector median EBITDA 9.8%" computed from 16-company sample. Some values were column-confusion errors
×Anonymous comparisons — "vs sector median" and "vs best-in-class" with no named peers
×Conceptually wrong levers — Working Capital treated as EBITDA lever (it affects ROCE). One-time items treated as recurring
×No external context — Intelligence derived solely from company filings with no market/regulatory overlay
→
After
✓Evidence-graded findings — Every lever tagged STRONG / MODERATE / DIRECTIONAL with specific data points and source citations
✓Named peer comparisons — "Crompton 9.8%, Havells 8.8%" from specific annual reports, not anonymous medians
✓Investigation Areas, not claims — "Here's what to investigate with strong evidence" instead of "Here's exactly how many bps you'll gain"
✓"Data Needed" per area — Each investigation area specifies what additional information validates the hypothesis
✓31-item news intelligence layer — Regulatory events, industry trends, macro factors, and company news with sources and dates

Core Philosophy

Evidence Strength Classification

Every finding is graded by evidence quality. No fabricated numbers — only what the data actually supports.

STRONG

Specific numbers from audited filings, earnings calls, or regulatory disclosures. Directly verifiable.

"CP capacity utilization 43% (10.8Mn installed vs 4.6Mn produced)" — FY25 Annual Report

MODERATE

Calculated from multiple data points or derived from management commentary with directional support.

"Segment margins diverge: EMP 8.8% vs UCP 6.2%" — Q2 FY26 Transcript analysis

DIRECTIONAL

Industry patterns, analogies, or management statements that suggest opportunity but need validation.

"Operating leverage from demand recovery" — Structural thesis, needs volume data
